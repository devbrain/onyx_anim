                           The Barber Pole

                     A CyberPainting by Timothy Early

   This is a CyberPaint sequence file.  Use Animate3.prg to view.  Set
for forward mode, buffering on, and a speed of 6-8, or whatever you
prefer.

   I created this animation using CAD 3D v.2.02, CyberControl, and
CyberPaint.   The rotating barber pole was my inspiration to create
a complete 3D barbershop.  This CyberPainting is the Barber Pole only.

  Working my way through the CyberControl tutorial sections, I came across
Tom Hudson's COILMAKE.CTL program.  I ran it a couple of times, making
several spiral springs.  But what could I do with a spiral spring?

  Using CyberControl, I cloned my last spring without moving it, and
rotated the copy 180 degrees.  I set my colors so that one spring was
red and the other white.  The endcaps were created with the spin tool,
and colored yellow.

  A simple CyberControl script rotated the pole through a complete
rotation.  I shot the scene twice, once in Outlined Draft, and again
in Final mode.  Looking at the resulting animations, I liked the caps
from the 'draft' version, but the pole from the 'final' rendering.

  CyberPaint was used to merge the outlined caps with the solid pole.
Some minor cleaning up followed, and text added.  An ADO effect for
my name was merged into the sequence.
