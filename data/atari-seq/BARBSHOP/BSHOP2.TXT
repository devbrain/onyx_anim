                         The Corner Barbershop

                     A CyberPainting by Timothy Early

   This is a CyberPaint sequence file.  Use Animate3.prg to view.  Set
for ping-pong mode, buffering on, and a speed of 6-8, or whatever you
prefer.

   I created this animation using CAD 3D v.2.02, CyberControl, and
CyberPaint.  This complex model features a one-chair barbershop with
a working mirror.

   Due to a combination of the viewpoint angle and camera motion, the
CAD 3D delta animation suffered from a severe case of the jaggies.  I
used CyberPaint extensively to minimize this effect between frames.

   For this animation, CyberControl was invaluable during the construction
process.  The whole concept began when I tried Tom Hudson's COILMAKE.CTL
script.  What could I do with a spiral spring?  I made two, rotated one
180 degrees about its long axis, put caps on the ends, and viola!, a
red and white barber pole!  Then I needed a wall to mount it on, and from
there, a whole barbershop.  CyberControl was used to align the various
pieces, clone objects in place, and move objects in precise increments.
